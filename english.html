2016 US Election Results: Trump clinches the White House in an unfortunate upset.

This marks a monumental change for American politics— and not in a good way.

By Noah Menikefs@noahmenikefsnoah@vox.com May 13, 2019, 8:20am EDT
Share Tweet Share
Share Election results 2016: Donald Trump wins the presidential election in shocking upset.
tweet share Reddit Pocket Flipboard Email

The events that unfolded last night marked a new era for the United States of America. Despite many controversial events leading up to the presidential election, Donald J. Trump will become the 45th US president.

Horrific Election Night

Currently, Mr. Trump, the real estate mogul and reality television star with no political experience is the winner of the election. Based on the information we have collected, he accumulated 306 electoral votes, 36 more than the required for presidency. Shockingly, Trump defeated highly qualified, former Secretary of State, Hilary Clinton. Around 2 a.m Clinton called Trump to concede and generously congratulate the billionaire for his victory. She became the 5th presidential candidate to win the popular vote but lose the election. Approximately an hour later, Trump delivered his victory speech to the public and is set to take the oath of office on January 20th, 2017. Later that evening, Clinton publicly conceded and advised worrying Americans to maintain an open mind for the unexpected presidency. She gratefully thanked all her Democratic supporters and wished them to accept the outcome. However, for many, this was not enough.

Controversial Campaign

Before his campaign, Trump was known for his position as a real estate tycoon and his reality television show, The Apprentice. He inherited his multi-billion dollar real estate company, The Trump Organization, from his hard-working father, Frederick Trump. On June 16th, 2015, Donald Trump formally declared his candidacy. Trump’s right-wing “Make America Great Again” campaign embodied the opposition to political correctness and Mexican immigration. Inevitably, it was met with extensive media coverage and mixed emotions from American citizens. Throughout his campaign, he made many radical claims such as his promise to build a wall to keep Mexicans from entering the country. Furthermore, he pledged to keep Muslims out of the country by inflicting a travel ban. Trump’s misogynistic, racist persona garnered the attention of many right-wing, republican nationalists. Most notably, in February 2016 David Duke, former Ku Klux Klan grand wizard, publically stated that voting for any candidate besides Trump “is really treason to your heritage.” Luckily, as the year passed by, Trump’s presidency seemed further from reality. It seemed as though each week he was exposed with a controversial quote made during a speech or throughout his past. Transcripts were released detailing Trump making extremely lewd comments regarding women. He continued to publish unfiltered tweets which pushed political correctness. To many, this was viewed as an abomination to American politics. To his fans, it was viewed heroically. Before the formal election, experts suggested a very modest lead for Hilary Clinton. They estimated she would defeat Trump with 297 electoral votes to his 241. Clearly, the pre-election polls were wrong. What does this suggest for the future of America?

Analysis of the Electoral System

Upon analyzing the electoral data, it can be somewhat unclear why Trump won. Hilary Clinton garnered 49.5% of the US votes, which amassed to 69,953,514. In comparison, Trump only received 42.7% of votes, amassing to 60,784,828. This highlights a possible fault in the American election system. The purpose of a democracy is to elect a leader which represents the wishes of the majority of citizens. In this scenario, the majority of American voters chose Clinton to be their president. However, due to our current electoral college system, Trump clinched the victory. Since Trump’s unfortunate victory, many experts have been quick to criticize our system for its misrepresentation of the American population. Nevertheless, current changes in the system will not undo the outcome finalized last night.

Public Reaction

I, like many other Americans, watched the occurrences last night asking myself: What the fuck is happening? How could my fellow Americans support an unqualified, misogynistic white supremacist? This was the same man who deemed all Mexicans as ‘rapists’ and boasted grabbing women by their genitals. Additionally, how could the pre-election polls be so undeniably incorrect? Personally, this suggests that many of Trump supporters were too embarrassed to publically admit their support due to the unfavourable media attention surrounding Trump. Consequently, his victory last night sparked an uproar throughout the country. Citizens quickly fled to social media to criticize the former reality television star. His controversial campaign inevitably led to many citizens having extreme resentment towards him. This resentment was felt through the influx of online posts condemning his past actions. Numerous individuals felt that he was unqualified due to his lack of government experience. Additionally, they criticized his unintelligent denial of climate change. Finally, some users pointed out that he is clearly unfit to run a country’s economy due to the fact that his hotel and casino businesses have been declared bankrupt six times between 1991 and 2009.

Conclusion

Donald Trump’s presidential victory marks a new era for the United States of America. An era in which minorities live in fear of their fellow Americans who are driven by the hatred spread by their president. A society which embraces the racist, xenophobic and misogynistic rhetoric used by their leaders. Personally, I fear for the females who are given orders by a man who battles accusations of sexual assault and brags about groping women. This ordeal has become an eye-opener for the entirety of our world that racism prevails in America.
